## The Operating Loop (memorize this)

For every piece of content Scott produces, run this loop. **One input becomes six outputs.**

```
Scott records 60-second video on a jobsite
    ↓
1. YouTube Short (uploaded vertical)
2. YouTube long-form companion (5–10 min, batched weekly)
3. Blog post (transcribed + expanded with diagrams)
4. Embedded on the relevant service page
5. Embedded on the relevant location page (if it's a city we serve)
6. $1/day Facebook/Instagram boost for 7 days targeting Central VA homeowners 35–65
```

Three videos a week × six outputs = 18 content surfaces per week. That's how a small contractor outranks national franchises.

## Production Cadence (Medium track — Justin's selection)

Per week:
- 2 to 3 short videos (60–90 seconds each).
- 15+ photos from active jobs.
- 1 blog post (transcribed and expanded from the strongest video).
- 1 batch of $1/day amplification on previous week's strongest piece.

Per month:
- 1 cornerstone blog post (1,500–2,500 words, deeply researched).
- 1 case study published.
- 1 location page expansion or refresh.
- 1 long-form YouTube video (5–10 min).

## Anti-Patterns (do these and we lose)

- **Templating location pages.** Same copy with city name swapped = HCU penalty.
- **AI-fluff blog posts.** Especially "Top 10" listicles with no original insight.
- **Stock photos for case studies.** Use real Scott photos or the case study doesn't run.
- **Buying reviews.** Google catches these; Angi catches these; trust erodes.
- **Targeting nonsense keywords.** "Best gutter contractor in the world" — we want intent, not vanity.
- **Deleting old content.** Update and improve instead. Old content with established backlinks is gold.

## Tracking

For each task, record in a project tracker (Asana, Notion, or a Google Sheet) the following:
- Task ID, owner, status, target publish date, actual publish date.
- Outputs created (YouTube link, blog URL, social posts).
- Performance after 30 days (views, position, leads attributed).
- 90-day position check.

Quarterly review: kill what's not working, double down on what is.
